Shoemaking Workshop

A hands-on course in traditional shoemaking, open to artists, makers, and creative people on Salt Spring Island.

Duration
5 Days
+ 1 day for last forming
Cost

The Wilding Foundation is offering a subsidized shoemaking workshop on Salt Spring Island. Over five intensive days, participants will learn the fundamentals of traditional shoemaking, from pattern drafting and leather cutting through lasting, stitching, and finishing. An additional day beforehand is dedicated to last forming, where each participant will prepare the custom lasts used throughout the course.

We are looking for artists, craftspeople, and other creative individuals, especially those with a background in making things by hand. No prior shoemaking experience is required. More details about the workshop schedule and materials will be posted here soon.

Commitment: Applicants must be available for all 5 days of the workshop, plus the additional last-forming day held beforehand. This program is made possible by the Wilding Foundation, which is covering the majority of the course cost so that it remains accessible to creative people regardless of financial means.
