FabLab

A creative haven at the heart of the community

The FabLab at the Salt Spring Public Library is a community makerspace equipped with 3D printers, laser cutters, design software, and more. It offers islanders of all ages a place to learn, experiment, and create -- all free of charge.

What began as a small, glass-walled room has grown into a thriving hub running six days a week at near-full capacity. In 2025, the FabLab logged over 1,100 appointments and 3,600 drop-in visits.

Through the FabLab, the Foundation also funds free Child & Youth STEAM programs covering robotics, coding, 3D printing, and drone piloting. Registrations are regularly waitlisted.

“The Fab Lab is a very special place for my neurodivergent child and for our community in general. It's not lost on me how lucky we are to have this amazing resource.”
GISS Robotics

Building robots, building futures

The Cyber/Scorpions team at Gulf Islands Secondary School brings together students from grades 8 to 12 who design, build, and compete through the annual VEX Robotics Competition.

The team has grown steadily, fielding up to 30 students and seven independently designed robots in a single season. The Wilding Foundation helps ensure students on Salt Spring have access to hands-on engineering experiences.

Catalyst Grants

Helping artists finish what they started

Grants of $3,000 to $10,000 for visual artists across the Southern Gulf Islands and area First Nations, administered with the Salt Spring National Art Prize. Over two years, $100,000 committed annually.

In its inaugural round, 16 artists were selected from approximately 45 applications, receiving funding to complete ongoing projects and explore new creative directions.
